### For Your Situation: **Start as Sole Trader, Transition to Company Later**

#### Phase 1: Validation/Bootstrap (Months 0-6)
**Structure: Sole Trader (ABN only)**

**Why Sole Trader First:**
| Factor | Sole Trader | Company |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Setup cost | Free (ABN) | ~$500-800 (ASIC) |
| Ongoing costs | Minimal | ~$300-1,000/year |
| Tax complexity | Simple (personal return) | Complex (corporate + BAS + ASIC) |
| Time to manage | ~2 hrs/month | ~5-10 hrs/month |
| Liability | Unlimited | Limited |
| Flexibility to experiment | High | Lower (formal structure) |

**When to Register:**
- Register ABN immediately when you start taking payments
- Must register for GST if turnover >$75,000/year (projected)
- Register business name (~$39/year) if using trading name ‚â† your name

**Tax Implications (Australia):**
- Income taxed at personal marginal rates (32.5% for $45k-$120k bracket)
- Can claim business expenses: home office, equipment, software, professional development
- Must pay quarterly PAYG instalments once income exceeds threshold
- Keep detailed records (required for 5 years)

#### Phase 2: Growth (Months 6-12+)
**Transition to Company When:**
- Annual revenue consistently >$80,000-100,000
- Hiring employees or taking on partners
- Need limited liability protection
- Tax advantages become worthwhile (corporate rate 25% vs personal 32.5-45%)
- Seeking external investment
- Planning studio space (asset protection critical)

**Company Structure Recommendation:**
- Proprietary Limited (Pty Ltd)
- You as sole director and shareholder initially
- Consider family trust as shareholder later (asset protection + income splitting)

## üöÄ PART 3: TIME-EFFICIENT VALIDATION STRATEGIES

### The "Weekend MVP" Philosophy
**Constraints = Advantages**
- Limited time forces ruthless prioritization
- You can't over-engineer if you only have weekends
- Forces focus on customer problems, not tech elegance

### Validation Framework: "Sell Before Build"

#### Week 1-2: Problem Discovery
**Goal: Confirm people have the problem AND will pay**

**Weekend Activities:**
1. **Day 1: Customer Interviews**
   - Find 10 people who might have the problem
   - 30-min conversations (Can be done during commute/evenings)
   - Key question: "What have you already tried to solve this?" (Checks for actual pain)

2. **Day 2: Landing Page MVP**
   - Build simple landing page (Carrd, Webflow, or plain HTML)
   - Describe the problem + your proposed solution
   - Include pricing (even if product doesn't exist yet)
   - Email capture: "Join waitlist" or "Pre-order now"

**Tools for Speed:**
- Landing page: Carrd ($19/year), Webflow (free tier)
- Forms: Tally.so (free), Typeform
- Payment capture: Stripe (no-code), Gumroad
- Email: Mailchimp (free tier up to 500 contacts)

#### Week 3-4: Pre-Sales Validation
**Goal: Get 3 people to commit to buying BEFORE building**

**The Pre-Sale Approach:**
1. Build a "Wizard of Oz" MVP ‚Äî manual behind the scenes, looks automated to customer
2. Offer founding customer discount (50% off lifetime)
3. Run the service manually for 3-5 customers
4. Document processes, pain points, time requirements

**Red Flags (Stop Here If):**
- Can't find 3 people willing to pre-pay
- Customers want wildly different things
- Problem isn't urgent enough ("nice to have" not "must have")

### Quick-Start Validation Channels

#### For B2B (Recommended for Software Engineers):
1. **LinkedIn Direct Outreach**
   - Target: People in your existing network + 2nd connections
   - Message: "I'm researching [problem]. Do you have 15 mins for a quick chat?"
   - 10-15% response rate typical
   - Can do during weekday evenings

2. **Industry Communities**
   - Slack/Discord communities in your target market
   - Reddit subreddits (r/startups, r/entrepreneur, industry-specific)
   - Indie Hackers, Hacker News "Show HN"

3. **Warm Introductions**
   - Ask friends: "Who do you know who struggles with [problem]?"
   - Former colleagues often your first customers

#### For B2C:
1. **Twitter/X Building in Public**
   - Share your journey
   - Build audience while validating
   - Network effect compounds over time

2. **Paid Ads (Small Budget)**
   - $100-200 on Meta or Google Ads
   - Test different value propositions
   - Measure: Cost per email signup

**Why Productized:**

|--------------|------------|--------|

| Warning Sign | Action |
|--------------|--------|
| Stress affecting health/relationships | Reduce workload; prioritize sustainability |
| Can't focus at day job due to side business excitement | Schedule side business time strictly; compartmentalize |
| Side business requires daytime client calls | Batch calls to lunch breaks; use async communication |
| Colleagues asking about "other projects" | Prepare diplomatic response; consider disclosure |
| Employer announces new product similar to your side business | Document timeline; legal review if overlap concerns |

### The "Go/No-Go" Decision Matrix

**Consider Transitioning to Full-Time When:**
- [ ] Side business revenue > 70% of salary for 3+ consecutive months
- [ ] Have 6+ months expenses saved as runway
- [ ] Side business has predictable, recurring revenue
- [ ] Legal/conflict review complete
- [ ] Clear growth path requiring full-time attention
- [ ] Health/relationships still intact

**DO NOT Transition When:**
- [ ] Revenue is one-time project work (not recurring)
- [ ] No savings buffer
- [ ] Employer is unaware and may have claims
- [ ] Side business relies on employer resources/knowledge
- [ ] Emotional decision (burnout, frustration) vs strategic
